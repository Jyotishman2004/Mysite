Manifesto

Bridging the gap between art & industry.

The Bauhaus movement began in 1919 when Walter Gropius founded a school with a vision of bridging the gap between art and industry by combining crafts and fine arts.

Prior to the Bauhaus movement, fine arts such as architecture and design were held in higher esteem than craftsmanship, but Gropius asserted that all crafts, including art, architecture and geometric design, could be brought together and mass-produced.
